Diversity Identity Core Engagement (DICE) Workshop

Exploring how we work in open source community together, with more awareness and understanding of our diversity
download .ZIPdownload .TGZ

This is a repository of materials for a workshop intended to explore and grow diversity awareness in the Mozilla project. The hope is that these tools and techniques could be useful in many contexts, both within technical communities and outside as well. This workshop's focus is people who are involved in an open source community, that has a specific mission, where participants (both paid and unpaid contributors) represent a wide variety of backgrounds and intersections of race, gender, class, sexual orientation, ability, religion, as well as other identities and core values.

The goal of this toolkit is to provide up to date materials from instances of the workshop taking place in various cities and communities so that we are iterating and improving the experience for participants. After each workshop is run we encourage participants to provide feedback in various forms, one of which is to file an issue here and/or make pull requests. Of course feedback by email or in person is also welcome if that is more comfortable.

We welcome and seek additional slide decks, new or alternate versions of the questions for the "Stepping Stones" exercise, localization of the current content, and feedback from workshop leaders on how their workshop went.
